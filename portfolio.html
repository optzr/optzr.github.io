What is Sharpe Ratio?

The Sharpe ratio measures risk-adjusted return by comparing excess return to volatility. Higher values indicate better risk-adjusted performance.

Why it matters

It helps you compare investments with different risk levels on an equal footing. A portfolio with 10% return and low risk might be better than one with 12% return and high risk.
